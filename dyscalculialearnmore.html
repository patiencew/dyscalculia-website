Counting

Dyscalculia can affect your ability to understand numbers in a variety of ways.  For some people, it might be harder to understand basic math concepts or make mental calculations. Others might struggle with reading data or understanding basic concepts like ratios, fractions, and percentages.

Dyscalculia can also make it difficult to manipulate numbers or understand the value of money.

Time

Dyscalculia can also affect your ability to understand and manipulate time.  For example, people with dyscalculia might struggle with calculating the time it takes for an event to happen or understanding deadlines.  They may also have difficulty interpreting the passage of time, like figuring out how long ago something happened or how much time has passed.

Directions

Dyscalculia can also lead to issues with navigation and direction.  People with dyscalculia can have difficulty understanding maps and directions, and often get lost in unfamiliar places. They may struggle with left and right directions and have trouble deciphering compass directions.

About Dyscalculator

Dyscalculator is a dyscalculia based learning site that strives to teach others about dyscalculia and spread awareness to this mathematical based learning disorder.

If you believe you may have dyscalculia, please reach out to our doctor or a therapist for possible assessments and/or accommodations.

Dyscalculia Facts

It's estimated that dyscalculia is at least 30 years behind in research compared to dyslexia!

Dyscalculia is also known as Developmental Dyscalculia!

In a class of 30 students, 1-2 may have dyscalculia.

While men and women both are diagnosed with dyscalculia in equal amounts, women are slightly more likely to be diagnosed with dyscalculia compared to men.
