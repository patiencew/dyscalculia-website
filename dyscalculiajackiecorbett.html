Jackie Corbett

"Dyscalculia didn't make me any less capable or hard-working,
and that was an important realization for me."

Dyscalculia didn't make me any less capable or hard-working, and that was an important realization for me. I was able to be more compassionate with myself and understand that my learning process was different from others.

That self-understanding allowed me to be creative and come up with my own strategies for studying math. Even if I needed extra time on a test or special accommodations, I was no longer worried about being judged by my peers or teachers.

I was proud of myself for embracing my dyscalculia and finding solutions that worked for me, and my success taught me that I could learn in my own way and still succeed.
