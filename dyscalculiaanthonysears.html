I had always felt like I was just being lazy and not trying hard enough at math. Â When I found out I had dyscalculia, I felt that it validated my struggles and gave me an appropriate explanation for why I found it so difficult.

But finding out about my dyscalculia didn't magically make the problem go away. I knew I still needed to work hard at math, but also had to do things differently. So, I started creating my own tools that would help me understand math concepts better and practice in ways that worked with my particular learning style.

For example, I'd use flashcards to memorize formulas and work on breaking down complex math problems into smaller parts that were easier to understand.I began to embrace my dyscalculia rather than fighting it, and that helped me make progress.
